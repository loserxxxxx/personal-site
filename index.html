The chronicle

One mod, an entire age

What changes

Advanced Medieval reshapes the overworld with biomes drawn from the age of stone keeps and iron crowns — rolling moors, sunken ruins, and ore veins that were never meant to be found. Every addition is built to feel like it always belonged.

Magic enters through the scroll: a single, elegant item that channels power without cluttering your hotbar with a dozen wands and trinkets.

What you'll find

New mobs roam the medieval biomes — some hostile, some willing to be tamed and ride beside you. Hand-placed structures hide loot, lore, and the first threads of the dungeons to come.

A brand-new dimension waits beyond the ordinary world, built entirely around this aesthetic — a destination, not a detour.

Inside the mod

Built in five pillars

Every system was designed to support the others — ores feed the forge, the forge feeds the scroll, and the scroll opens the dimension.

I

Medieval biomes

New terrain types woven into the overworld, each with its own flora, palette, and atmosphere.

II

Ores & resources

Rare materials exclusive to the mod, unlocking new tiers of weapons, armor, and tools.

III

The scroll

A single item that carries the entire magic system — read it, channel it, master it.

IV

Mobs & companions

New hostile threats and tamable allies that fit naturally into the medieval setting.

V

Lost structures

Hand-built ruins and outposts scattered across the world, hiding loot and lore.

VI

A new dimension

An entire realm built around this theme — explorable once you're ready for it.

VII

Crafted weapons

New blades and armaments forged from the mod's own ores, tuned for medieval combat.

VIII

Wood & plants

Decorative flora and timber that bring authenticity to medieval builds and landscapes.

Before you begin

Requirements & installation

Requirements

  • Minecraft version 1.21.1
  • Mod loader NeoForge
  • Dependency Pehkui
  • Latest build v1.1.0

Installation steps

  • Install NeoForge for 1.21.1
  • Download Pehkui and Advanced Medieval
  • Move both .jar files to mods folder
  • Launch and enter the new world
